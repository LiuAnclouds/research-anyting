# Academic Writing Standards

## Prohibited Constructions

- "To the best of our knowledge" — Either verified by systematic search, or do not make the claim.
- "Significantly outperforms" without specific quantity — Replace with "achieves X% higher [metric]."
- "We prove" without formal proof — Replace with "We demonstrate" or "We show."
- "Very", "completely", "extremely", "dramatically" — Remove or replace with specific measure.
- "Recent years have witnessed..." — Replace with specific temporal claim with citations.
- "It is well known that..." — Provide citation or remove.
- "Due to the fact that" — Use "Because."
- "It can be seen that" — Remove; state observation directly.

## Required Constructions

- Active voice: "We propose..." not "It is proposed that..."
- Concrete quantities with uncertainty: "0.072 +/- 0.003 AUC-ROC improvement (p < 0.01, Wilcoxon)"
- Specific conditions: "When the homophily ratio exceeds 0.3" not "on heterophilic graphs"
- Falsifiable statements traceable to specific experimental results

## Figure Standards

- Vector formats (PDF, SVG) for all non-photographic figures. Raster at >=300 DPI for photos.
- Sans-serif font (Arial or Helvetica), minimum 8pt tick labels, 10pt axis labels, 12pt titles.
- Colorblind-friendly palette. Avoid red-green as sole distinguishing feature.
- Every figure must have labeled axes with units and be referenced in text.
- Prohibited: pie charts, 3D effects on 2D data, default software color schemes.

## Table Standards

- Self-contained: understandable without referencing text.
- Best results: bold. Second-best: underline.
- Standard deviations must accompany means.
- Numbers decimal-aligned. Statistical significance annotations explained.
